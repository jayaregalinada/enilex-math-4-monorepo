# Credits

This project ([MIT](./LICENSE)) bundles third-party and original assets. Every
committed media asset must appear here with its source and licence **before** it
is committed — see [ADR 0006](./docs/adr/0006-authored-audio-assets.md). Audio
files are gitignored until they have an entry here.

Third-party assets may only be committed under a licence that **permits
redistribution** in this MIT repository. Third-party work is never attributed as
original.

## Audio — background music

`apps/rounding-numbers/src/assets/music/`

Two groups: tracks the project **owns** (AI-generated — committable) and
**third-party** tracks used only as local dev assets (not committed).

### Owned — AI-generated with Treblo (committable, MIT)

Generated by the project owner with **[Treblo](https://treblo.com)**
(Sonauto, Inc.). Per Treblo's [Terms of Service §8 "Outputs"](https://treblo.com/tos),
Treblo **assigns all rights in the generated Outputs to the user**, with **no
usage or redistribution restrictions and no attribution required** (confirmed for
the free tier in the [FAQ](https://treblo.com/landing#faq): *"We place no usage
restrictions on songs you create, and whatever rights may exist in them belong to
you, not us."*). They are therefore the owner's work, released under this repo's
[MIT licence](./LICENSE) with the rest of the project. This satisfies ADR 0006's
"original work by a contributor" path.

> Treblo's grant applies *"as long as what you put into them is yours"* — the
> prompts/lyrics fed in are the owner's own, not third-party copyrighted material.
> Purely AI-generated audio may carry thin or no copyright, which only widens the
> freedom to use and redistribute it.

### Third-party — local dev use only (NOT committed)

These play in local development but are **not committed**: the licence permits
using the music *inside* a work, yet **forbids redistributing the raw audio file**
— and committing an `.mp3` to this public MIT repo *is* standalone redistribution.
They stay gitignored; the synthesized fallback (`packages/audio` → `MUSIC_TRACKS`)
covers any pool left empty (currently the Hard pool).

### Adding more committable music

Use tracks whose licence **permits redistribution**: **CC0/public domain** (no
attribution, still credit as a courtesy), **CC-BY** (redistribution *with*
attribution — record the credit here, the single place for it), **original work**,
or **AI output you own** (as above). Redistribution-friendly sources include
[OpenGameArt](https://opengameart.org) (CC0/CC-BY) and
[incompetech](https://incompetech.com) (Kevin MacLeod, CC-BY 4.0).
